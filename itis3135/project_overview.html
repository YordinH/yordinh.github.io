Project Overview

Application Description and Purpose

The proposed application is a marketing and information website for Queen City Fitness Coaching, a small personal training and fitness coaching business based in Charlotte. The purpose of the website is to clearly explain the coach’s services, training philosophy, and example programs, and to make it easy for visitors to contact the coach to request a consultation or ask questions. The site will also include basic educational content that helps beginners feel more comfortable starting a fitness plan.

Intended Users of the Website

  • Local beginners: People in the Charlotte area who are new to working out and want safe, guided plans.
  • Busy students and professionals: Users who have limited time and want efficient, tailored workouts and coaching.
  • Intermediate lifters: Users who already go to the gym but want help with programming, accountability, or improving technique.
  • Remote clients: Users who are not in Charlotte but are interested in online coaching, check-ins, and written programs.

Overview of the Website Content

The website will consist of at least five main pages (this proposal plans for six), including:

  • A Home page that introduces Queen City Fitness Coaching and highlights the main calls to action.
  • An About page that explains who the coach is, what their qualifications are, and their training philosophy.
  • A Services page that describes the different coaching options offered.
  • A Training Programs page that shows example read-only workout structures and uses interactive filters or tabs.
  • A Success Stories & Gallery page that displays testimonials and an interactive photo gallery.
  • A Contact page with a form that lets visitors request a consultation or ask questions.

The site will also include interactive elements such as an image gallery, form validation, a jQuery UI widget, and AJAX-based loading of sample program data. All dynamic functionality will be read-only, in line with the course requirement that no CRUD storage is implemented.

User Categories and Examples

The main categories of users who will view and interact with this web application are listed below, along with example scenarios.

  • Local beginners Example: a first-year college student in Charlotte who feels overwhelmed by the gym and wants a simple, beginner-friendly workout plan and coaching.
  • Busy professionals Example: an office worker who has only 3–4 days per week to work out and wants efficient, structured sessions.
  • Remote clients Example: a former Charlotte resident who moved but wants to continue receiving online programs and check-ins.
  • Returning clients Example: someone who previously worked with the coach and is visiting the site to request an updated program or ask a follow-up question.

Content Categories

The content of the site will be organized into the following categories:

  • People: Information about the coach (bio, certifications, photo).
  • Services: Descriptions of 1-on-1 coaching, small-group sessions, and online coaching.
  • Training programs: Example weekly structures and workout outlines (read-only).
  • Events / logistics: General notes on scheduling, session length, and how coaching works.
  • Goals and philosophy: The overall goal of the business and how the coach approaches long-term progress.
  • Gallery of photos: Training environment photos, client success visuals (where appropriate), and motivational images.
  • Contact information: Form, email, and simple location description.

Page Design

For each page in the site map, the following sections describe the page name, purpose, audience, content, data entry, validation, interactive elements, and any special notes.

1. Home: index.html

  • Purpose: Welcome users, introduce the brand, and direct them toward key actions such as viewing services or requesting a consultation.
  • Audience: All users, especially first-time visitors.
  • Content: Hero banner with a short tagline, brief summary of the coaching approach, highlight cards for main services, and a call-to-action button.
  • Data entry: None.
  • Validation: Not applicable.
  • Buttons/links: “View Services”, “Explore Programs”, “Request Consultation”, plus main navigation links.
  • Actions: Buttons navigate to other pages or scroll to specific sections on the home page.
  • Special notes: May include a simple JavaScript smooth-scroll effect or rotating highlight content.

2. About: about.html

  • Purpose: Provide background about the coach, their experience, and their training philosophy, and answer frequently asked questions.
  • Audience: Users who want to learn more about who they might be working with.
  • Content: Coach bio, photograph, list of certifications, and an FAQ section.
  • Data entry: None.
  • Validation: Not applicable.
  • Buttons/links: Links to services, programs, and contact pages.
  • Actions: FAQ content will be presented as a jQuery UI accordion so users can expand and collapse individual questions.
  • Special notes: This page will showcase a jQuery UI widget.

3. Services: services.html

  • Purpose: Describe each coaching service (1-on-1, small-group, online) in more detail so users can compare options.
  • Audience: Users deciding whether the services match their needs and budget.
  • Content: Service cards with descriptions, what is included, session length, and general pricing information.
  • Data entry: None directly.
  • Validation: Not applicable.
  • Buttons/links: “Learn more”/“Request consultation” buttons that lead to the contact page.
  • Actions: Clicking buttons will navigate to the contact page or scroll to a short contact prompt.
  • Special notes: Simple JavaScript hover or selection states may highlight a chosen service.

4. Training Programs: programs.html

  • Purpose: Show example read-only training structures (e.g., strength, fat loss, general fitness) to demonstrate how the coach organizes workouts.
  • Audience: Users who want to see concrete examples of programming.
  • Content: Cards or tables describing sample weekly schedules and workouts.
  • Data entry: Optional filter controls (buttons or dropdowns) to select the type of program to view.
  • Validation: Not needed, because no user data is stored.
  • Buttons/links: Filter/tabs for different goals and a link to the contact page.
  • Actions: JavaScript and/or jQuery will update the displayed programs when filters are selected. AJAX will be used to load program descriptions from a local JSON file (read-only).
  • Special notes: All data interaction will be read-only to comply with the course restriction on CRUD operations.

5. Success Stories & Gallery: gallery.html

  • Purpose: Display client success stories and an interactive photo gallery to build trust and motivation.
  • Audience: Users looking for social proof and real examples of results.
  • Content: Short testimonials, before/after-style descriptions (if appropriate), and training-related photos.
  • Data entry: None.
  • Validation: Not applicable.
  • Buttons/links: Clickable image thumbnails and a “Contact the Coach” link.
  • Actions: JavaScript or a jQuery plugin will provide an interactive gallery (e.g., lightbox or modal) so that clicking a thumbnail opens a larger version with a caption.
  • Special notes: This page will satisfy the assignment’s requirement for a gallery or interactive images.

6. Contact: contact.html

  • Purpose: Provide a simple way for users to request a consultation or ask questions.
  • Audience: Users ready to reach out, plus current clients who need to send a quick message.
  • Content: Contact form, brief instructions, and an email/location summary.
  • Data entry: Fields such as name, email, preferred contact method, fitness goal, and a message text area.
  • Validation: Client-side validation in JavaScript (required fields, email format, minimum message length).
  • Buttons/links: “Submit Request” button and navigation links.
  • Actions: When the form is submitted, JavaScript will validate the inputs. If valid, a confirmation message is shown on the page (no server-side storage).
  • Special notes: This page will meet the requirement to include forms for collecting information while remaining read-only/presentational from a backend perspective.

Dynamic Functionality on the Website

The web application will include several forms of dynamic functionality that make use of JavaScript. All interactions will respect the course constraint that the site does not perform full CRUD operations or store user data on a server.

  • Contact form with validation (Contact page): The contact form will use JavaScript to validate required fields, check email format, and display feedback messages to the user.
  • FAQ accordion (About page): A component will allow users to expand and collapse frequently asked questions, improving readability and organization.
  • Interactive program filters: Program summaries will be loaded from a local JSON file and then displayed based on the user’s selected goal or category.
  • Interactive image gallery (Success Stories & Gallery page): Clicking on thumbnails will open a larger image in a modal/lightbox view, potentially using a jQuery or JavaScript plugin.
  • Smooth scrolling and highlight effects (Home and Services pages): Simple JavaScript enhancements will provide smooth scrolling to sections and visual highlighting when users interact with buttons and navigation links.
