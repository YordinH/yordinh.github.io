Website Evaluations

Evaluating the CRAP design principles.

Apple | apple.com

Apple’s homepage shows a minimal, product first layout with clear hierarchy, good whitespace, and typography that emphasizes clarity and focus.

  • Contrast: Strong light/dark panels and bold product imagery create focal points and clear hierarchy.
  • Repetition: Reusable modules, consistent button styles, and typographic reinforce brand unity across sections.
  • Alignment: A tight grid keeps headlines, imagery, and CTAs aligned, producing an orderly flow down the page.
  • Proximity: Related text and CTAs are grouped near their product imagery, while spacing separates distinct product stories.

JamesBond‑Shop | jamesbond-shop.com

A site for a James Bond museum/shop that mixes all sorts of bad design. The page structure feels inconsistent and dated, which makes scanning hard.

  • Contrast: Low visual hierarchy. There are many similarly styled links and text blocks reduce visual help and make calls to action hard to spot.
  • Repetition: Inconsistent visual patterns, like mixed fonts, colors, and button/link treatments, weaken recognition and cohesion.
  • Alignment: Irregular alignment and column link lists create a jagged reading path. Also elements don’t share a clear grid.
  • Proximity: Unrelated links are crowded together while related items aren’t clearly grouped, increasing messy feel.
